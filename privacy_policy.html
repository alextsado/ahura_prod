Personal data we collect

Ahura collects data from you, through our interactions with you and through our product. You provide some of this data directly, and we get some of it by collecting data about your interactions, use, and experiences with our product.

Technical and Usage Information. Whenever you use the Ahura plug in, we may collect "Non-identifying Information" from you, such as your referring URL, browser, operating system, and Internet Service Provider. Without a subpoena, voluntary compliance on the part of your Internet Service Provider, or additional records from a third party, this information alone cannot usually be used to identify you.

Specific Information Collected Per Study Session. This data is collected only within the specified time of your "study session" (e.g.Starting when you enter a topic). After the study session ends or is cancelled, the Ahura product does not collect any information. Information the Ahura product collects during your study session is strictly confined to the data specified below:

  • The URL of each site visited on the Chrome browser.
  • Keywords specified in the study search. Keywords gleaned from each page visited.
  • The first 700 characters of content after the first heading on each page visited during the study session.
  • The time at which the page is visited.
  • The amount of time spent on each page visited.
Please note that all urls, keywords, and page contents collected during the study session are encrypted at rest, and that access to all data is restricted and secured.

Personal data we do not collect

Personally Identifiable Information. We do not collect certain "Personally Identifiable Information" from you when you sign up to our Services, that can be used to identify you, such as your name, email address, mailing address, telephone number, and any other information. We do not mine stored keywords for your personal data. In addition, we do not tie our data to any individual. Meaning, your data is collected anonymously by not connecting your identity to the data we collect from the Ahura product.

Our product requires some personal data to provide you with a service. If you choose not to provide data necessary to provide you with a product or feature, you cannot use that product or feature. Where providing the data is optional, and you choose not to share personal data, features like personalization that use such data will not work for you.

Information Sharing. All data collected will not be sold to third party companies or shared with any Ahura partnerships at this time. If that should change it will be noted in an updated privacy policy that will then be shared with users of the Ahura product.

Cookie Policy. At this time we do not use cookies with the Ahura product. We do not, read webpage cookies, set webpage cookies, and we do not store cookies for any period of time. As such, we are not using session cookies, preference cookies, advertising cookies, or security cookies. These preferences are being stored in your browser's personal data storage, this is why you are asked to allow storage permission during product set up.

How we use your data

We use the data we collect to provide you with rich, interactive experiences in the Ahura product. In particular, we use data to:
  • Generate reports that provide you insight into your completed study sessions. This information includes which keywords you were focused on during your study and how long you studied those topics.
  • Provide you with a list of websites that were relevant to your study session.
  • The 700 characters of content collected during your study sessions are stored in case of future NLP calibration.

Data retention

We will only keep your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

To determine the appropriate retention period for personal data, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the personal data, the potential risk of harm from unauthorized use or disclosure of your personal data, the purposes for which we process your personal data and whether we can achieve those purposes through other means, and the applicable legal requirements.

By law we have to keep basic information about our customers, such as payment data, generally for seven years after they stop being customers for tax purposes. Please note that we may retain information that is otherwise deleted in anonymized and aggregated form, in archived or backup copies as required pursuant to records retention obligations, or otherwise as required by law.
